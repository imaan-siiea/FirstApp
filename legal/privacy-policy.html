VoterIQ Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 4, 2026  ·  Last updated: June 4, 2026

VoterIQ ("the app", "we", "us") is a nonpartisan civic information app operated by SIIEA. It helps you find your ballot, learn about candidates and representatives, locate polling places, and understand how to register and vote. This policy explains what we collect, why, who we share it with, and the choices you have.

The short version: We collect only what the app needs to show you civic information — primarily an address you type in and (if you create an account) your email. We do not sell your data, we do not serve ads, and we do not use advertising or analytics tracking SDKs. The app does not access your device's GPS or precise location.

1. Information we collect

Information you provide

DataWhen & why
Address (street address you type) Used to look up your voting districts, ballot, candidates, and the nearest polling place. You enter it manually — the app never reads your device location. If you are not signed in, your address stays only on your device. If you sign in, it may be saved to your account so you can return to your ballot.
Email address and password Only if you choose to create an account (optional). Passwords are stored as a salted hash (bcrypt) — we never store or see your plaintext password.
AI chat messages Questions you type into the AI ballot guide, plus your address for context, are sent to our AI provider to generate an answer. Don't include sensitive personal details in chats.
Follows & reminders The politicians, states, or parties you choose to follow, and election reminders you opt into, so we can show your alerts and notify you of relevant updates.

Information collected automatically

DataWhen & why
Push notification token If you enable notifications, your device's Expo push token and platform (iOS/Android) are stored so we can deliver election reminders and alerts you've opted into. You can turn notifications off in your device settings at any time.
Basic technical/log data Our servers record standard request information (e.g. IP address, request time, and endpoint) for security, rate-limiting, and debugging. These logs are retained for a limited period and are not used to build advertising profiles.

2. How we use your information

  • To show your ballot, candidates, representatives, and polling places for your address.
  • To answer your questions through the AI ballot guide.
  • To create and secure your optional account and keep you signed in.
  • To send election reminders and alerts you have opted into.
  • To operate, protect, and improve the service (security, rate-limiting, debugging).

We do not use your information for advertising, and we do not sell or rent it.

3. How information is shared

We share data only with the service providers needed to deliver civic information, and only the minimum required:

We may also disclose information if required by law, to enforce our terms, or to protect the rights and safety of our users and the public.

4. Data retention

If you create an account, we keep your account data until you delete your account or request deletion. Address data tied to your account is retained to power your saved ballot until you remove it or delete your account. Server logs are kept only for a limited operational period. If you use the app without an account, your address is not stored on our servers beyond the live request needed to answer it.

5. Your choices and rights

  • Use without an account: Most features work without signing in.
  • Notifications: Enable or disable any time in your device settings.
  • Access & deletion: Request a copy of, or deletion of, your account data by emailing us (see below). We will delete your account and associated data on request.
  • Location: The app does not request or use device location permissions.

6. Security

We use industry-standard measures including encrypted transport (HTTPS), hashed passwords, and encrypted on-device storage for authentication tokens. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.

7. Children's privacy

VoterIQ is intended for eligible and prospective voters and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, an in-app notice.
